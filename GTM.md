# Go-To-Market (GTM) Execution Strategy

This framework outlines the zero-dollar tactical acquisition pipeline designed to scale user acquisition for the EQ AI spend audit engine, positioning it as a high-conversion lead generation tool for Credex optimization consultations.

---

## 1. High-Intent Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
The exact target user is a **VP of Engineering, Head of Infrastructure, or Technical Co-Founder** at a **Seed to Series A software startup (15 to 50 employees)**. 

### Why this specific segment:
*   At this stage, software development teams face sudden tool sprawl from overlapping developer tools (such as mixed deployment models of Cursor, GitHub Copilot, and ChatGPT Team accounts).
*   Unlike enterprise corporations with dedicated procurement offices, technical leaders at this stage manage their own department software budgets directly.
*   They are highly motivated to optimize their operational software spend to stretch their runway before opening their next venture funding round.

---

## 2. Pre-Intent Search Queries & Content Friction Points
Right before a user needs this tool, they are actively looking for solutions to cost creep or license allocation headaches. Their search behavior follows these specific digital tracks:

### Direct Google Search Queries:
*   `"how to manage duplicate cursor and github copilot licenses"`
*   `"reduce developer chatgpt team subscription costs"`
*   `"startup tooling sprawl optimization checklist"`
*   `"cutting infra overhead without reducing engineering headcount"`

### Digital Consumption Patterns:
They routinely scan technical performance post-mortems, platform runtime reduction guides, and crowd-sourced SaaS savings spreadsheets on platforms like Hacker News. They also read trending threads tracking the monthly operational cost of AI tokens.

---

## 3. Targeted Professional Digital Ecosystems
To capture these buyers efficiently, outreach will target niche, high-density technical networks rather than broad public platforms:

*   **Subreddits:** `r/devops` (focusing on license tracking), `r/SaaS` (focusing on architecture stack reduction), and `r/startups` (focusing on operational runway management).
*   **Slack Communities:** *DevOps Collective*, *Launch House Alumni*, *Y Combinator Founder Slack* (specifically the `#ops-and-finance` channel).
*   **Discord Servers:** *Vercel Community*, *Supabase Community Discord*, and *Together AI Open-Source Infra Hub*.
*   **X (Twitter) Lists:** Private, curated operator directories tracking active technical leaders, specifically targeting profiles matching the keywords *"VP Eng"* or *"Head of Infra"* at newly funded startups.

---

## 4. Zero-Budget 30-Day First 100 Users Playbook
This precise weekly playbook relies entirely on targeted content sharing and direct outreach:

*   **Days 1–7 (The Data Trap):** Publish a detailed guide titled *"The Silent $400/Dev Leak: Over-indexing on Modern AI Coding Seats"* on Hacker News and Indie Hackers. The post will feature real price data from `PRICING_DATA.md`. It will link directly to the app as a free, un-gated calculator to check their team's vulnerability. **Target: 15 users.**
*   **Days 8–14 (Direct Network Outreach):** Run an automated scraping loop on LinkedIn targeting technical founders who raised a seed round 6–12 months ago. Send 25 direct messages daily using an informational angle: *"Hey [Name], saw the round. We built a free script for our portfolio teams to instantly audit duplicate Cursor/Copilot licenses. Takes 30 seconds to copy/paste your seat counts if you want to look at it."* **Target: 35 users.**
*   **Days 15–22 (Communities Native Engineering):** Find active questions about software budget cuts in communities like `r/devops` and the YC Slack. Post high-value text breakdowns detailing tier degradation math, adding the tool's public stateless link (`/share/...`) as a visual example. **Target: 30 users.**
*   **Days 23–30 (Viral Proof Loops):** Share anonymized screenshots of top audit results on X, tracking real data insights: *"Just audited a 30-person engineering team. Discovered $7,200/year in duplicate seat licenses. Check your team's configuration instantly."* **Target: 20 users.**

## 5. The Unfair Distribution Channel
The unfair advantage belongs to **Credex’s native ledger positioning**. By embedding this audit engine directly into the onboarding workflow for companies signing up for Credex corporate cards or expense management tools, every new user automatically audits their AI tool stack. 

Credex can also partner with top startup accelerators (such as Techstars or target seed funds) to offer the tool as a mandatory step in their portfolio runway optimization checklists. This gives the application a highly authoritative, zero-cost acquisition loop that competitors cannot match.
